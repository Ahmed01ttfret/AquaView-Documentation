User Guide

1. File Entry

This section explains how to upload your data files into AquaView.

AquaView accepts files in the .xlsx and .csv formats. To upload your data, follow these steps:

  1. Navigate to the Import tab.
  2. Click the Add button to upload a file.

The uploaded file must follow the following structure:

  • The file must include a column labeled ID (this can be in any column of the file).
  • A column for Date/Time should also be included.
  • All columns, except the ID and Date/Time, should contain numerical values or null values.

Important: Import will be unsuccessful if the ID column is missing. Additionally, in-depth analysis cannot be performed without a Date/Time column.

2. Configurations

Learn how to set up and configure AquaView to suit your preferences and needs.

Porting Data:

Select Data Source:

  • Click the "Source of Water" button on the "Import" tab.
  • Choose your water source from the provided dropdown list.

Define Columns:

  • Select the columns containing "Date" and "ID" from the respective dropdown menus.

Click "Continue": Proceed to the next step.

Important Notes:

Data Validation:

Import will fail if:

  • Incorrect configurations are made.
  • Non-numeric data is found in columns other than "Date" and "ID".
Water Quality Index (WQI) Calculation:

To enable WQI calculation, follow these steps:

  • Click the "Configuration" button.
  • Define which parameters correspond to specific parameters in the standards.
WQI Configuration:

Standards:

The system uses predefined standards. A default standard named "Parameters" is included. To customize or add standards:

  • Go to the "Settings" page and click "Parameters and Standards."
  • Click the "+" button on the app bar to add a new standard.
Standard Format:

Each standard entry should follow this format:

        [parameter, unit, ideal, weight]

Example: ["pH", "units", 7, 0.2]

  • parameter: Name of the parameter.
  • unit: Unit of measurement.
  • ideal: Ideal concentration value.
  • weight: Significance weight of the parameter in WQI calculation.

3. Home Page

Overview of the home page layout and available navigation options.

The Home Page is the central hub of AquaView, where you can view the most important aspects of your water quality data. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of the key features:

Imported Data:

The home page displays the data you‚Äôve uploaded. This includes a table showing the values for each parameter, as well as an overview of the dataset. The table will allow you to quickly check the data, and you can easily access additional analysis features.

Chart of Missing Values:

On the home page, you will also see a chart that visually represents the missing values in your dataset. This allows you to quickly identify any data gaps and make necessary adjustments before further analysis.

Distribution Charts:

In addition to the missing values chart, AquaView provides distribution charts (such as histograms or density plots) for each parameter. These charts help you visualize the spread and distribution of your data, allowing for better insights into trends and anomalies.

With all these features, the home page serves as a comprehensive starting point for your water quality analysis, providing both an overview and tools for deeper exploration.

4. Trends

This section covers how to analyze trends in your data, such as time series and patterns.

The "Trends" page in AquaView is designed to help you visualize and analyze changes in your data over time, and uncover potential patterns. Here are the key visualizations available:

Time Series Chart:

The Time Series chart allows you to view how specific water quality parameters change over time. By plotting the data points based on the date or time column, you can identify any temporal trends or fluctuations in water quality.

Scatter Plot:

The Scatter Plot displays the relationship between two parameters, allowing you to easily identify correlations or patterns in the data. This is useful for understanding how different water quality parameters interact with each other over time.

Heat Map:

The Heat Map provides a visual representation of the state of each ID over time. It uses colors to show variations in the data, making it easy to identify trends, outliers, or irregularities in the dataset. This can be particularly helpful in spotting changes in water quality across different locations or times.

Time Series of Water Quality Index (WQI):

A special Time Series chart is dedicated to visualizing the WQI over time. This chart helps track the overall water quality of your dataset and assess whether it meets predefined standards. It is an important tool for monitoring changes in water quality and identifying areas that may need attention.

By using these tools, you can gain deeper insights into the trends and patterns within your water quality data, allowing for more informed decision-making and analysis.

5. Insight/AI Note

Explore the AI-powered insights and recommendations generated by AquaView based on your data.

AquaView is equipped with **Gemini AI**, a powerful tool that analyzes your water quality data and provides valuable insights. This AI-driven feature summarizes the state of your water, making it easier for you to understand the overall quality and key trends without manual analysis.

Summarizing Water Quality:

Gemini AI examines your dataset and generates a concise summary of the water's condition. It highlights important aspects such as:

  • Overall water quality status (e.g., good, moderate, poor).
  • Key parameters affecting water quality.
  • Potential concerns or areas requiring attention based on the data trends.

Asking Questions:

With the interactive **Q&A** feature powered by Gemini AI, you can ask specific questions about your data. Whether you're looking for insights on trends, parameter correlations, or specific water quality assessments, you can easily engage with your dataset in a conversational way. Just type your question, and the AI will provide you with relevant answers and recommendations.

This feature enhances the user experience by making data analysis more accessible and intuitive, allowing you to make informed decisions based on AI-powered insights.
